OTT & ECT development — five strands

The induction/training programme covers: 1) Culture — UK school norms, HAP expectations, what SLT-carried behaviour culture asks of a classroom teacher. 2) Pedagogy — UK T&L expectations vs Jamaican training: routines, questioning, checking understanding, independent practice (the 30-min benchmark). 3) Language — classroom register, instructional language, EAL-aware communication. 4) Professionalism — safeguarding duties, standards, accountability, parents. 5) Effective quality of education — curriculum thinking, assessment, adaptation. Deliver with Dina co-leading; Jessica/Asia classrooms as live models.

Training themes: why am I here as a teacher · the purpose of a curriculum — the why, not the what · the curriculum as the progression model · intent → implementation → impact · conceptual progression · owning and being proud of your curriculum.

SEF rewrite — deadline Tue 30 Sept

Commissioned by Peter Groves; CB is sharing the template. Must address the new Ofsted evaluation areas and toolkit language — and because inspectors now agree focus areas from the school's own self-evaluation, the SEF effectively writes the inspection agenda. Feed the ALG self-grading sessions into it so it's a collective product, not a solo one. The AIP gaps (Year 7 recruitment, Humanities) get filled in the same exercise.

Curriculum & Teaching — inspection reference list

The old "Quality of Education" judgement is replaced by Curriculum and teaching + Achievement. Summarised expectations (swap in exact toolkit wording once the handbook arrives):

Curriculum: ambitious for all pupils, including SEND and disadvantaged · coherently planned and sequenced towards clear end points · builds cumulatively — the curriculum is the progression model · subject-specific concepts and knowledge progress logically · reading prioritised · leaders and teachers own the curriculum's rationale.

Teaching: secure subject knowledge · clear presentation of new material · systematic checking of understanding and addressing misconceptions · recall and practice that build long-term memory · adaptive teaching that meets needs without lowering ambition · high expectations and sustained independent practice.

Assessment: used to check learning and inform teaching · not an excessive workload burden · prepares pupils reliably for national assessments without narrowing the curriculum.

Achievement (linked area): pupils know more and remember more over time · strong outcomes in national tests and qualifications · pupils are ready for their next stage. Remember secure fit: every strand, every classroom.
